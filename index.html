Welcome to About me

My name is Felipe Silva. I ws born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. I am a fan of the football club Fluminense. I lived in Rio for 8 years before moving to the United States. I first moved to New York City and lived in Astoria, Queens. I enjoyed my time in New York but I don't really enjoy the winter, I prefer the summer. I got to see the Statue of Liberty and Times Square while i lived there. I moved to Florida when I was 12 years old and I love it here. My favorite time of the year is summer. My two favorite hobbies are going to the beach and playing video games. I spent most of my formative years here in Tampa, FL. I attended many of the schools here to include Sligh Junior High school, Greco Junior High school, Adams Junior High school, and Tampa Bay Technical High School. I was a swimmer in high school and I was also in the marching band. After High school I was not ready for college, I did not prepare to my fullest ability and would ultimately fail in my endeavors.

I chose to join the military, specifically the United States Army. When I joined the army I chose to be a biomedical technician at the time the military occupational specialty(MOS) was 91A. I went on to complete basic combat training at Fort Leonardwood, Missouri. After completing basic training I was assigned to Foxtrot company 187th Med at Whichita Falls, Texas. I spent about 1 year training to be a biomedical technician for the army. I learned basic and advanced electronics to complex systems like radiological systems and ventilation systems. My first duty station was Fort Hood, Texas where I spent most of my military career and whilst with Echo company 4th Forward Support Battalion, I deployed to Kuwait and Iraq in 2003. In 2005 I made the decision to leave the military and continue my career as a biomedical technician.

I spent a short time as a phlebotomist working what was then Florida Blood Services which is now One Blood, before continuing to further my career as a biomedical technician. I was able to use many of the skills I learned in my time in the military, specifically the skills I learned being a part of a medical company in the army to apply myself in the medical industry.

In 2006 I attained a position at Tampa General Hospital as a Biomedical Electronic Technician in one of Tampa Bay's best hospitals and Level 1 trauma center. I learned way more than I could have imagined during my 10 years at TGH. I went on to get many certifications. and I specilized in Anesthesia and Ventilation. I enjeyed my time at TGH but I knew that the industry was changing and required me to pursue more education. I decided to journey to college in 2012 to pursue a different career path. I attented Hillsborough Community College and University of South Florida.
